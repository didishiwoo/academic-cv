abstract: "Temporal interference stimulation (TIS) enables non-invasive deep brain stimulation, but conventional isotropic conductivity models lack accuracy in white matter regions. This study integrates magnetic resonance electrical conductivity tomography with diffusion tensor imaging and the NODDI model to construct personalized anisotropic conductivity models. Using COMSOL finite element simulations with three hierarchical conductivity models and five 10-10 electrode configurations, we demonstrate that anisotropic modeling yields approximately 20% differences in electric field intensity in white matter regions and over 10% differences in gray matter and whole-brain regions compared to isotropic models."
